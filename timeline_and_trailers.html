Development Timeline

In 2016, Yang Qi, the art director, proposed the project Black Myth: Wukong. But the team thought it was too early for them to start this project.

In 2017, after the success of Art of War: Red Tides, Game Science started the development of Black Myth: Wukong. The progress was slow due to lack of manpower.

On Aug. 19, 2020, the team released the first trailer in order to recuirt more personnels. Surprisingly, the 13 minute gameplay trailer went viral: it got 45 million views on bilibili, a video site in China, and 10 million views on youtube. In a few days, Game Science received more than 10000 resumes from all around the world, while they planned to hire only 100.

In March 2021, Game Science accepted the investment from Tencent Games, but under the condition that Tencent Games doesn't seek control of the company. On Aug. 19 2021, the second trailer was released. This was the first trailer after the team switched the game engine from UE4 to UE5. It was again a 13-minute gameplay trailer, in which the powerful physics engine of UE5 brought excellent collision effects in the snow scene.

On Aug. 19 2022, the third trailer was released. Unlike the previous two trailers, this was a 6-minute plot trailer. Game Science demonstrated their advanced technologies in facial capture, motion capture, and bone rigging on humanoid enemies. The expected release date of the game is no earlier than 2023.
